Comparing Compliance Laws

The data protection regulation is different in every country. For example, the rules of the GDPR are quite different from the Colombian data protection. The GDPR is the toughest privacy and security law in the world (GDPR, 2018). For this reason, the Colombian data protection law is compared with the GDPR. The main authority of data protection in Colombia is the Superintendence of Industry and Commerce (SIC). Colombian law sets out eight data protection principles: Legality regarding data processing, purpose, freedom, quality, transparency, access and restricted circulation, security, and confidentiality (SIC, 2020). On the other hand, the GDPR sets out seven data protection principles: lawfulness, fairness and transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity and confidentiality (security) and accountability.

The securing personal data principle of the Colombia law: “The information subject to Treatment by the person responsible for the Treatment or Processor referred to in the law, must be managed with the technical, human and administrative measures that are necessary to provide security to the records, avoiding their adulteration, loss, consultation, unauthorized or fraudulent use or access.”(SIC, 2020).

Both Colombian laws and the GDPR have the principle of security but this principle can be affected by security breaches of personal data. Personal data security breaches are regulated within each of the laws. In Colombian law, 15 days are established to notify the competent entity, whereas in the GDPR it must be notified 72 hours after the incident. However, if you do not notify within the hours established in the GDPR, you must explain why you did not do so, whereas under Colombian laws you must not give any explanation. On the other hand, under Colombian laws and the GDPR, if the incident that occurred is not a risk to the rights and freedoms of people, it will not be necessary to make a notification of the incident. Finally, under the GDPR, if a security breach of personal data is likely, the controller must notify the interested party of the incident without delay. However, under Colombian law, you are not required to do so.(Gutierrez, 2021)
